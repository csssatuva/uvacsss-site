UVA Housing Options

Understanding your housing choices at the University of Virginia

üè† First-Year Housing

All first-year students are required to live on-Grounds in residence halls

  • Housing Application: Submit through SIS by the deadline (typically May 1st)
  • Residence Halls: Alderman, McCormick, and Hereford residential areas
  • Room Types: Traditional doubles, suite-style, and apartment-style options
  • Meal Plans: Required for all first-year students living on-Grounds
  • Living Learning Communities: Special interest communities available

üè´ On-Grounds Housing

University-owned housing for upperclass students

  • Upperclass Residence Halls: Brown, IRC, and specialty housing
  • Apartments: Copeley Hill Graduate Housing, family housing options
  • Application Process: Apply through Housing Division in spring
  • Benefits: Close to campus, utilities included, community programming
  • Costs: Varies by location and room type, meal plans optional

üèòÔ∏è Off-Grounds Housing

Private housing options in Charlottesville area

  • Popular Areas: JPA (Jefferson Park Avenue), Belmont, Preston, Rugby Road
  • Housing Types: Apartments, townhouses, shared houses
  • Lease Timing: Most leases start in August, sign by February/March
  • Transportation: Consider bus routes, parking, and walking distance
  • Utilities: Typically separate from rent (electricity, water, internet)
  • Resources: UVA Off-Grounds Housing website, local real estate agents

Housing Timeline & Tips

üìÖ Important Dates

  • First-Year Housing: Application due May 1st
  • Upperclass On-Grounds: Applications open in February
  • Off-Grounds: Start searching in January, sign by March
  • Graduate Housing: Apply early, limited availability

üí° Pro Tips

  • Budget: Factor in utilities, parking, and transportation costs
  • Roommates: Use UVA housing portals or social media groups
  • Location: Consider proximity to your classes and activities
  • Safety: Research neighborhood safety and lighting

Detailed Housing Information

üè† First-Year Housing Details

Residential Areas

  • Alderman Road: Traditional residence halls, close to dining
  • McCormick Road: Suite-style living, newer facilities
  • Hereford Residential College: Unique living-learning community

Room Options

  • Traditional Double: Two students share a room with hall bathroom
  • Suite Style: Multiple rooms connected with shared bathroom
  • Apartment Style: Multiple bedrooms with kitchen and living area

üè´ On-Grounds Options

Upperclass Halls

  • Brown Residential College: Close to classes, dining options
  • IRC (International Residential College): Multicultural community
  • Specialty Housing: Language houses, themed communities

Graduate & Family Housing

  • Copeley Hill: Graduate student apartments
  • Family Housing: Options for students with families
  • Application: Separate application process, early deadlines

üèòÔ∏è Off-Grounds Neighborhoods

Popular Student Areas

  • JPA (Jefferson Park Avenue): Close to campus, many apartments
  • Preston Avenue: Shopping centers, bus routes
  • Belmont: Trendy neighborhood, restaurants and shops
  • Rugby Road: Traditional student housing area

Housing Search Tips

  • Start Early: Begin searching in January for August move-in
  • Visit Properties: Schedule tours before signing leases
  • Read Leases Carefully: Understand terms, deposits, and fees
  • Transportation: Check bus routes and parking availability
